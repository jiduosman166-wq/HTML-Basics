rooted in both my academic training and my lived experiences in a region where economic systems are often disrupted by instability and limited infrastructure.
Growing up in Nuba Mountains region of Sudan, I witnessed how fragile supply networks can significantly affect communities.
Essential goods such as food, medicine, educational materials and humanitarian aids often fail to reach those who need them most due to inefficient logistics and weak distribution systems.
These experiences shaped my intellectual curiosity about how better designed supply chains can strengthen economies and improve people’s lives.
